# Olist E-Commerce SQL Business Case



SQL-based business analysis of a Brazilian e-commerce marketplace using the Olist dataset (Sep 2016 - Aug 2018) from Kaggle.



## Overview



This project explores Olist's order, revenue, delivery, and customer data across 9 relational tables loaded into MySQL. I wrote business-focused SQL queries to answer key questions a data analyst would be expected to answer — revenue trends, seller performance, delivery rates, payment behaviour and customer satisfaction by category.

The goal was to think like a business analyst: not just query the data, but pull out insights that actually mean something for the business.



## Business Questions Answered



- How did revenue and order volume grow year over year?

- Which months had the highest sales, and why?

- Which product categories drive the most revenue?

- Who are the top performing sellers?

- How well does Olist deliver orders on time?

- How do customers prefer to pay?

- Which states have the most customers?

- Which categories have the best customer satisfaction scores?



## Key Insights



- Revenue grew from \~46K in 2016 to \~8.4M in 2018,a strong year-on-year growth.

- November 2017 spike is clearly Black Friday driven.

- Bed, bath \& table and health \& beauty are the top revenue-generating categories.

- Top seller made \~$505K from just 973 orders,the highest revenue per order, likely premium products.

- 91.9% of orders were delivered on time.

- Credit card dominates at 74% of all transactions.

- Sao Paulo alone accounts for \~42% of all customers.

- Bed & bath has the highest orders but below-average satisfaction which is a potential quality issue worth investigating.
